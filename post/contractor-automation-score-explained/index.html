The Contractor Automation Score: A 100-Point Diagnostic for Your Business

We built a FICO-style scoring system that tells contractors exactly where their revenue leaks are. Five pillars, one hundred points, zero guesswork.

Mar 25, 2026 7 min read

Why We Built a Scoring System

Every contractor we talk to knows they are leaving money on the table. But almost none of them can tell you where. "We need better marketing" is the default answer — but marketing is not the problem when 60% of your leads never get a follow-up.

We needed a diagnostic tool that could pinpoint the exact revenue leaks in a contracting business. Something objective. Something actionable. Something that gives a business owner a clear picture of where they stand in under 3 minutes, with specific recommendations they can act on immediately. Our Zapier Elimination case study shows what acting on those recommendations looks like in practice.

So we built the Contractor Automation Score — a 100-point assessment modeled after FICO scoring. Five operational pillars, twenty points each, twelve targeted questions. The score tells you exactly where your revenue is leaking and how to fix it.

The Five Pillars

The score evaluates five operational areas, each worth 20 points. These five pillars represent the five highest-leverage automation opportunities in any service business — the exact areas where the gap between manual processes and automated systems translates directly to revenue.

1. Speed to Lead (0-20 pts)

How fast do you respond to new inquiries? Under 5 minutes scores high. Over 24 hours scores near zero. This single metric predicts close rate better than anything else we measure.

The research is unambiguous: responding within 5 minutes makes you 21x more likely to qualify a lead. After an hour, your odds drop by over 90%. We score this aggressively because the revenue impact is disproportionate — a contractor who fixes speed to lead alone typically sees a 15 to 25% increase in booked jobs.

What we look for: Do you have automated instant response (text + email within 60 seconds)? Do you have internal team notifications with lead context? Do you have after-hours response capability? Do you have a self-booking calendar link in your initial outreach? (For the complete breakdown on why this matters, see our Speed to Lead deep dive.)

2. Quote Follow-Up (0-20 pts)

After you send a quote, what happens? If the answer is "we wait for them to call back," you are losing 40-60% of winnable jobs. The quote does not sell itself. The follow-up does.

We evaluate whether you have automated follow-up sequences, how many touches are in the sequence, whether the timing is optimized (24 hours, 72 hours, 7 days), and whether the sequence stops automatically when the quote is accepted or declined. Manual follow-up that depends on someone remembering to call scores low. Automated multi-touch sequences with behavioral triggers score high.

The revenue math here is significant. A business sending 40 quotes per month at a $5,000 average value that adds a 3-touch follow-up sequence typically recovers 20 to 30% of previously abandoned quotes. That is 4 to 6 additional closed jobs per month — $20,000 to $30,000 in recovered revenue.

3. Review Generation (0-20 pts)

How many Google reviews do you have? Are you actively generating them after every completed job? What is your review velocity — how many new reviews per month? Is the process automated or manual?

Reviews are the number one trust signal for local services. Businesses with 50+ reviews earn 266% more leads than those with fewer than 10. A 0.1-star improvement in rating can boost conversion by 25%. Yet most contractors have a handful of aging reviews and no system to generate more.

We score based on total review count, average rating, review velocity (new reviews per month), whether you have an automated request system connected to your FSM, and whether you have negative sentiment interception (routing unhappy customers to private feedback before they post publicly). The full methodology is in our Review Automation Playbook.

4. Pipeline Visibility (0-20 pts)

Can you tell me right now how many leads are in your pipeline, what stage they are in, and which ones need attention? If the answer lives in someone's head instead of a system, that is a leak.

Pipeline visibility is about knowing, at any moment, exactly where every lead and quote stands. How many new leads this week. How many quotes are outstanding. Which quotes are going stale (sent more than 5 days ago with no response). What the conversion rate is by source. Which stage has the biggest drop-off.

We score based on whether you have a centralized pipeline view, whether it updates in real time from your FSM, whether you have stale lead alerts, and whether you can segment by source, stage, and value. Contractors who score high on pipeline visibility almost always score high on the other pillars too — because visibility is what enables optimization.

5. Customer Reactivation (0-20 pts)

What happens after the job is done? Most contractors never talk to past customers again. But reactivation campaigns — seasonal reminders, maintenance offers, referral requests — are the cheapest revenue you will ever generate.

Acquiring a new customer costs 5 to 7x more than retaining an existing one. Your past customer database is a goldmine — every one of those customers has neighbors, friends, and family who might need the same service. A quarterly outreach to your database consistently generates 5 to 15 warm leads per month at zero acquisition cost.

We score based on whether you have automated seasonal campaigns, anniversary check-ins, referral programs with incentives, and maintenance reminders. A business with 500 past customers and zero reactivation strategy is leaving $50,000 to $100,000 per year in repeat and referral revenue on the table.

What Your Score Means

  • 80-100 — Automation Leader: You are running a tight ship. You are ahead of 90% of contractors in your market. Fine-tuning at this level adds margin and competitive moat. The conversation shifts from "fix leaks" to "how do we build an unfair advantage?"
  • 60-79 — Growth Ready: Solid foundation, but 2-3 leaks are costing you real revenue. You have some automation in place but gaps remain. Typical scenario: good speed to lead but no quote follow-up, or strong reviews but no reactivation. Closing these gaps is straightforward and the ROI is immediate.
  • 40-59 — Opportunity Zone: Significant gaps. You are likely losing 30-40% of potential revenue to manual processes. Half your growth engine is running on autopilot and the other half is running on hope. The good news: every fix at this level has dramatic revenue impact.
  • 20-39 — At Risk: Your competitors who automate these five areas are actively winning your jobs. Leads are leaking, quotes are dying, reviews are stagnating, and past customers are hiring someone else. The gap between your current revenue and your potential revenue is measured in six figures.
  • Below 20 — Critical: Your growth is bottlenecked by systems, not demand. Fixing this is the highest-ROI move you can make — higher than any marketing spend, any new hire, any equipment purchase. The systems gap is so large that closing even one or two pillars will have immediate, measurable impact on revenue.

The Revenue Impact Calculator

Your score is not just a number — it translates directly to dollars. Based on your answers, your average job size, and your monthly lead volume, we calculate an estimated revenue impact for each pillar:

  • Speed to Lead gap: If you are losing 25% of leads to slow response at $3,500 average job value and 80 leads per month, that is approximately $70,000 per month in addressable revenue
  • Quote Follow-Up gap: If 45% of your quotes go unfollowed at $5,000 average quote value and 35 quotes per month, that is approximately $79,000 per month in recoverable revenue
  • Review gap: If competitors with 200+ reviews are capturing leads that should be yours, the indirect cost is 10-15% of your total inbound volume
  • Pipeline gap: If you cannot identify stale leads and bottlenecks, the efficiency loss is 10-20% of your sales capacity
  • Reactivation gap: If your database of 500+ past customers generates zero repeat or referral business, you are leaving $4,000 to $8,000 per month on the table

The total gap for a typical mid-size contractor scoring in the 30 to 50 range is $15,000 to $50,000 per month in revenue that their existing marketing already generated but their systems failed to capture.

How the Score Drives Action

The score is designed to be actionable, not just informational. Each pillar maps to a specific set of automations that can be implemented on top of your existing FSM:

  • Low Speed to Lead score → implement instant response automation (text + email within 60 seconds)
  • Low Quote Follow-Up score → deploy 3-touch automated follow-up sequence
  • Low Review score → wire review requests to job completion triggers with negative sentiment routing
  • Low Pipeline score → set up real-time pipeline dashboard with stale lead alerts
  • Low Reactivation score → launch quarterly customer database campaigns

None of these require replacing your FSM. None require retraining your team. They sit on top of your existing stack and automate the revenue work that your field service platform was never built to handle.

The assessment includes 12 scored questions across the five pillars plus 4 intake questions (your FSM platform, crew count, annual revenue, and biggest operational headache) that help us personalize the recommendations.

Every contractor who has taken the assessment has been surprised by at least one finding. The gaps you know about are rarely the ones costing you the most. The score makes the invisible visible — and once you can see the leak, you can fix it.
