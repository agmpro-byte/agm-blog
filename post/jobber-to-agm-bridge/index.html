How We Connected Jobber to a Full Growth Engine — Without Replacing Anything

Texas Turf runs on Jobber. They didn’t want to switch. So we built a bridge that syncs jobs, triggers follow-ups, and generates reviews automatically.

Mar 22, 2026 5 min read

The Challenge

Texas Turf is a growing artificial turf installer based in Texas. They run their entire operation on Jobber — scheduling, quoting, invoicing, the works. Their crews are efficient. (For the full case study with results, see our Texas Turf deep dive.), their work quality is strong, and their reputation in the market is solid. They had no intention of switching platforms.

But Jobber does not do growth automation. No automated follow-ups after quotes. No review requests after jobs. No way to nurture leads who are not ready to buy today. No missed call recovery. No customer reactivation campaigns. They were doing all of that manually — which meant most of it was not getting done.

The result was a business that was operationally excellent but commercially underperforming. Leads came in and sat for hours. Quotes went out and were never followed up. Jobs were completed with no review request. Past customers never heard from them again. The marketing was generating demand, but the systems were not converting it.

This is the story we hear from almost every Jobber-based contractor we work with. Jobber handles the job lifecycle brilliantly. Everything around the job — the growth engine — is left to manual processes that always fall behind.

The Solution

Instead of replacing Jobber, we built a bridge. AGM Pro Tools sits alongside Jobber and handles everything it does not:

  • New leads from web forms, Google Ads, LSAs, and referrals flow into AGM automatically via webhook and API integration
  • Speed-to-lead automation fires within 60 seconds of a new inquiry — personalized text message, detailed email with portfolio photos, and internal notification to the sales team with lead context
  • Quote follow-ups trigger when a Jobber quote is sent — a 3-touch sequence at 24 hours, 72 hours, and 7 days, with automatic stop when the quote is accepted or the customer responds
  • Missed call text-back fires within 30 seconds of any call that goes to voicemail — "Sorry we missed you, reply here or book a time that works"
  • Review requests go out automatically when a job is marked complete in Jobber — satisfaction check first, then Google review link for happy customers, private feedback form for anyone who had an issue
  • Customer reactivation campaigns run quarterly to the entire past customer database — seasonal tips, maintenance offers, referral incentives
  • Pipeline visibility gives the owner a real-time view of every lead's status, quote progress, and conversion metrics across all sources

The Technical Integration

The bridge between Jobber and AGM is built on Jobber's GraphQL API. When events happen in Jobber — a new quote is created, a job status changes, an invoice is paid — the data flows into AGM in real time. The integration is bidirectional: when a lead responds to an AGM follow-up sequence, the activity is visible in Jobber's client notes.

The key design principle: zero disruption to the existing workflow. The crews never see AGM. They open Jobber in the morning, see their schedule, complete their jobs, mark them done. Everything that AGM does happens behind the scenes. The owner sees both systems, but the field team only sees Jobber.

This was non-negotiable for Texas Turf. They had trained their crews on Jobber over two years. Any solution that required learning a new system or changing daily habits was dead on arrival. The automation layer had to be invisible to everyone except the business owner.

The Results

Within the first 60 days of deployment:

  • Response time went from 4+ hours to under 2 minutes — every lead gets an instant, personalized touchpoint regardless of when it comes in
  • Google reviews increased from 47 to 120+ — automated review requests after every completed job, with follow-up for non-responders
  • Quote conversion rate improved by 28% — the 3-touch follow-up sequence recovered quotes that would have died in silence
  • Missed call recovery rate: 31% of voicemails converted to text conversations and booked appointments
  • Monthly revenue grew by $18,000 — with the same ad spend, same crew size, same marketing budget
  • Zero change to daily Jobber workflow — crews did not know AGM existed

Where the Revenue Came From

The $18K monthly increase broke down across three primary sources:

  1. Speed-to-lead conversions ($8,200/month): Leads that previously would have called a competitor while waiting for a response were now engaged within 60 seconds. The 34% increase in lead-to-appointment rate translated directly to additional booked jobs.
  2. Quote recovery ($6,100/month): The automated follow-up sequence at 24/72 hours/7 days recovered 22% of quotes that previously went unanswered. These were not lost to competitors — the homeowners simply forgot or got busy. A well-timed reminder was all it took.
  3. Review-driven leads ($3,700/month): Going from 47 to 120+ reviews improved their Google Map Pack positioning significantly. Inbound call volume increased by approximately 15% over the same period, directly attributable to improved search visibility and trust signals.

None of this required additional marketing spend. The leads were already there. The quotes were already sent. The jobs were already completed. The automation layer simply ensured that every downstream revenue opportunity was captured instead of lost to manual gaps.

The Review Transformation

The review growth deserves special attention because it illustrates the compound effect of automation. Before AGM, Texas Turf had 47 reviews — accumulated over years of occasionally asking customers. After the automated review system was deployed:

  • Month 1: 18 new reviews (more than the previous 6 months combined)
  • Month 2: 24 new reviews
  • Month 3: 31 new reviews
  • Average rating maintained at 4.9 stars (the satisfaction routing caught 3 unhappy customers and routed them to private feedback before they posted publicly)

By month 4, they had broken 120 reviews and were consistently appearing in the Google Map Pack for "artificial turf installation" queries in their market. Their inbound lead volume increased by 15% with zero additional ad spend. (For the complete review automation methodology, see our Review Automation Playbook.)

What Did NOT Change

This is equally important. Here is what stayed the same:

  • Crews continued using Jobber exclusively — no new logins, no new apps, no new training
  • The scheduling workflow was untouched — same dispatch process, same job management
  • Invoicing and payment collection remained in Jobber — AGM does not handle financial transactions
  • The marketing channels were unchanged — same Google Ads, same LSA budget, same referral network
  • No new hires were made — the automation replaced the equivalent of a part-time follow-up coordinator

The only visible change for the business owner was a dashboard showing real-time pipeline status, lead response metrics, and review velocity. Everything else ran silently in the background.

The Takeaway

You do not need to replace your field service software to grow. You need to plug the gaps around it. That is exactly what AGM Pro Tools is designed to do — connect to your existing stack and handle the growth systems your FSM was never built for.

Jobber handles the job. AGM handles everything around the job — the first touchpoint, the follow-up, the review request, the reactivation. Together, they create a complete revenue engine where no lead, no quote, and no customer falls through the cracks.
